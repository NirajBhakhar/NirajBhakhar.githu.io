Program Overview

The M.Sc. in Data Science at the University of Regina provides a rigorous curriculum designed to equip students with foundational and advanced skills in computer science, data analysis, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Our program offers students hands-on experience through projects, industry collaborations, and research opportunities, ensuring they are well-prepared for real-world challenges.

Our interdisciplinary approach blends elements of statistics, computer science, and domain expertise to create a well-rounded data science professional. Students can customize their learning experience with a diverse selection of electives and specialize in key areas like machine learning, big data, or data visualization. With access to faculty members who are industry leaders, students will not only gain technical expertise but also learn to navigate the ethical and professional aspects of working with data.

Customizable Course Curriculum

CS 700: Advanced Algorithms (3 Credits)

Study advanced concepts in algorithms, including optimization, graph theory, and computational complexity. This course emphasizes both theoretical and practical aspects of algorithm design, preparing students to solve complex real-world problems using techniques like dynamic programming, divide and conquer, and approximation algorithms.

CS 710: Data Mining and Machine Learning (3 Credits)

Explore various data mining techniques, supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms, and an introduction to deep learning. Topics include decision trees, clustering algorithms, neural networks, and ensemble methods using popular frameworks like Python's scikit-learn and TensorFlow. The course includes hands-on labs to analyze and interpret data patterns.

CS 711: Statistical Methods in Data Science (3 Credits)

Learn statistical techniques including hypothesis testing, regression analysis, and statistical inference, crucial for effective data analysis. This course focuses on using statistics to solve real-world problems, emphasizing practical skills with R and Python libraries, including NumPy, Pandas, and SciPy for statistical computing.

CS 712: Data Visualization (3 Credits)

Gain hands-on experience in visualizing data using popular tools such as Tableau, Matplotlib, and Power BI. Learn to communicate complex data-driven insights to both technical and non-technical audiences effectively. Topics include data storytelling, visual perception, and creating interactive dashboards and visual reports.

CS 713: Big Data Analytics (3 Credits)

Understand and implement big data technologies such as Hadoop and Spark to analyze large datasets efficiently. Learn about distributed computing principles, NoSQL databases, and techniques for processing petabytes of data in parallel. The course includes hands-on labs to work with large datasets in cloud environments.

CS 714: AI for Data Science (3 Credits)

Learn about artificial intelligence methods including natural language processing (NLP), reinforcement learning, and computer vision, and how to apply them in data science contexts. This course includes practical experience building machine learning models using TensorFlow and Keras to solve real-world problems.

CS 715: Deep Learning for Computer Vision (3 Credits)

Introduction to neural network architectures and deep learning frameworks used for image recognition, detection, and classification tasks. Topics include convolutional neural networks (CNNs), transfer learning, and advanced architectures such as ResNet and YOLO. Hands-on labs include building models to process and classify images.

CS 716: Natural Language Processing (3 Credits)

Learn how to process and analyze natural language data using state-of-the-art machine learning techniques and NLP libraries like SpaCy and NLTK. Topics include tokenization, sentiment analysis, named entity recognition (NER), and language modeling. The course involves practical exercises on text analysis and building chatbots.

CS 717: Cloud Computing for Data Science (3 Credits)

Introduction to cloud-based data storage and processing using AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure, with an emphasis on data science applications. Topics include serverless computing, cloud databases, and using cloud-based machine learning services. The course includes labs on deploying data pipelines and machine learning models in the cloud.

CS 718: Ethics in Data Science (3 Credits)

Examine ethical issues in data science, including privacy, bias in machine learning, and responsible data usage in different industries. Topics include data governance, algorithmic fairness, transparency, and the societal implications of AI technologies. The course includes case studies on ethical dilemmas faced by data scientists in the industry.

Admissions

To apply for the M.Sc. in Data Science, prospective students must meet the following requirements:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, Statistics, or a related field.
  • Strong foundation in programming (Python, R) and statistics.
  • GRE scores (optional but recommended).
  • Two letters of recommendation from academic or professional references.
  • Statement of purpose explaining your career goals, motivations for studying data science, and relevant experience.
  • Updated resume highlighting relevant academic projects, work experience, and technical skills.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are the application deadlines?

The application deadlines for Fall and Spring semesters are March 31 and September 30, respectively.

Are GRE scores required for admission?

GRE scores are optional but recommended for a strong application.

What is the typical class size?

Classes in the program typically have 25-30 students, ensuring personalized attention and learning.

Are there any financial aid opportunities available?

Yes, we offer scholarships, assistantships, and paid research opportunities. Contact the financial aid office for more details.

What career opportunities are available for graduates?

Graduates can pursue roles in data analytics, machine learning, consulting, research, and more at top tech companies, financial institutions, and research labs.
